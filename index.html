About me

Hi! I am Konstancja Gałat, a bioinformatician with a strong background in molecular biology. I graduated from Jagiellonian University with a bachelor's degree in biotechnology and a master's degree in molecular biotechnology. Currently, I am pursuing a PhD at IIMCB Warsaw, where I study the gut-liver axis by analyzing multi-omic data, including single-cell RNA sequencing, metabolomics, and metagenomics.

During my undergraduate studies in biotechnology at Jagiellonian University, I developed a keen interest in molecular biotechnology, which led me to pursue a master's degree in the same field. My academic journey has provided me with a deep understanding of molecular processes and their applications in various biological contexts.

In my previous research, I developed pipelines for genomic (phage annotation) and metagenomic (soil mycobiome) analysis using Snakemake and Nextflow. Throughout my academic career, I have built a strong foundation in bioinformatics, allowing me to efficiently handle large-scale datasets, perform complex data analyses, and generate meaningful visualizations.

In my current research at the Laboratory of Cellular Genomics at IIMCB Warsaw, I specialize in analyzing the gut-liver axis. By employing advanced bioinformatics tools and statistical methods, I aim to unravel the diversity, composition, and interactions between these two organs.

Projects

Liver fibroblast analysis

Predicting Increased Blood Pressure Using ML in Python

Prediction of increased blood pressure by body mass index (BMI), waist (WC) and hip circumference (HC), and waist hip ratio (WHR) using a machine learning (SVM and CART model).

Age-related Study of MSI and ADHD in R

Investigation of the potential cognitive and behavioral distinctions between older individuals with ADHD and MCI, exploring the hypothesis that age-related cognitive decline may compound preexisting deficits in ADHD patients. The project's objectives involve creating a predictive model for disease based on the psychological profiles of patients, showcased through an interactive shiny application using a separate dataset for individuals with ADHD and ASD.

It was analysis of my own data from fluorcam. I was focused on finding differences in photosynthesis between plants with different genotypes and different treatments.
