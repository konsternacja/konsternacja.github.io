About me

Hi! I am Konstancja Gałat, a bioinformatics with a strong skills related to data analysis, visualiations and statistics. I am a graduate of the Jagiellonian University, where I studied biotechnology (bachelors) and molecular biotechnology (masters). My research is focused on the analysis of the mycobiome of soil samples. I am interested in the analysis of microbial communities and their impact on soil health and ecosystem dynamics. Throughout my academic journey, I have acquired a solid foundation in bioinformatics, enabling me to proficiently handle large-scale datasets, perform complex data analyses, and generate meaningful visualizations.

During my undergraduate studies in biotechnology at Jagiellonian University, I developed a keen interest in molecular biotechnology, which led me to pursue a master's degree in the same field. My academic endeavors provided me with a deep understanding of molecular processes and their applications in various biological contexts.

In my current research, which I conduct with Pawel Labaj Lab, I specialize in the analysis of the mycobiome, focusing on soil samples. By employing advanced bioinformatics tools and statistical methods, I aim to unravel the diversity, composition, and functional potential of fungal communities within the soil ecosystem. This research contributes to our understanding of the intricate relationships between soil microorganisms and their ecological roles.

Projects

In my projects I am focused on developing my coding skills in Python and R, but also on learning new tools and frameworks. I am interested in data analysis, modelling and visualization. I am also interested in machine learning and deep learning.

Predicting Increased Blood Pressure Using ML in Python

Prediction of increased blood pressure by body mass index (BMI), waist (WC) and hip circumference (HC), and waist hip ratio (WHR) using a machine learning (SVM and CART model).

Age-related Study of MSI and ADHD in R

Investigation of the potential cognitive and behavioral distinctions between older individuals with ADHD and MCI, exploring the hypothesis that age-related cognitive decline may compound preexisting deficits in ADHD patients. The project's objectives involve creating a predictive model for disease based on the psychological profiles of patients, showcased through an interactive shiny application using a separate dataset for individuals with ADHD and ASD.

It was analysis of my own data from fluorcam. I was focused on finding differences in photosynthesis between plants with different genotypes and different treatments.
